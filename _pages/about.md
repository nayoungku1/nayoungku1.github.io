### "See the Invisible, Do the Impossible."

I am an undergraduate student at [Handong Global University](https://www.handong.edu/eng/), studying Life Science & Applied Artificial Intelligence, where I build foundational machine learning models to decode the language of biology. Previously, I obtained a BA in Psychology at the National Institute for Lifelong Education when I was a high school senior.

I view the genome as a sophisticated language system where representation learning can uncover regulatory patterns hidden within its context. My central research question is how we can learn a unified representation from biology's complementary languages: the linear text of the genome and the visual context of cellular images. Rather than searching for isolated biomarkers, I aim to build generative architectures that map the latent regulatory grammar of the 'ATGC vocabulary.' By capturing the inherent heterogeneity of biological data, I hope to engineer robust, open-source tools that empower the broader scientific community to navigate the unseen.

### Research Interests
* Representation Learning for Genomics
* Multimodal Integration & Unified Representations
* Causal & Interpretable Deep Learning
* Self-Supervised Learning in Bioinformatics
